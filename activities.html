Activities

Geothermal Spas & Hot Springs

We went to four different spas: Laugarvatn Fontana, the Blue Lagoon, Reykjadalur Natural Hot Springs, and the Sky Lagoon. The natural hot springs was a crowd favorite as the river was located in a beautiful valley that we had to hike up to. The other spas were man-made, but heated with geothermal energy. We enjoyed Lagarvatn Fontana and the Sky Lagoon because they were more interactive and were more scenic. The Blue Lagoon was nice as well, but more touristy and the silica in the water didn't feel very pleasant.

Glacier Hike & Lagoon Kayaking

We did a glacier hike in the Vatnajokull area. This activity was guided, and the company provided the necessary safety gear (helmet, crampons, harnesses, etc.). Other than the vague directions the company gave to arrive at the location, the hike was great! The entire experience was about 3 hours, with 1 hour actually on the ice. I would have enjoyed spending more time on the glacier. As for kayaking in Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon, the activity was cancelled due to bad weather, and large pieces of ice blocking the path.

Golden Circle

The Golden Circle is about a 200 mile drive going through some Iceland's most popular attractions. We first went to Thingvellir National Park where we saw unique rock formations and the continental drift between the North American and Eurasian plates. We then went to Gullfoss, which is a large waterfall, comparable to Niagara Falls. We also visited Haukadalur Valley, where we saw active geysers.

Volcano Hike

The volcano hike was by far one of my favorite activities. It is currently erupting, and conditions are constantly changing. We were able to see the lava fields, and flowing, glowing magma. We also hiked for about an hour to see the crater, and saw the lava geysers. The weather was not ideal, but was still a very euphoric, and once in a lifetime experience.
